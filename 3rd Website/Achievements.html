first woman of chinese decent to serve as a pilot in the Royal Canadian Air Force Reserves, around 1945: first Chinese-Canadian woman to graduate in law at UBC, in 1953, at the age of 26: first Chinese-Canadian woman to be admitted to the bar in Canada in 1954: first Chinese-Canadian to practice law in BC, Canada: first Chinese-Canadian woman to practice her own law in 1955

Margaret Gee statements in interviews:

There was a lot of anti-Chinese racism at the time, espcially in BC. Even for educated Chinese-Canadians, succeeding in Canada was difficult nonetheless.

Margaret Gee mentioned the racism she faced in her school, university, and in her work place. She noted that she had been fortunate for such incidents were relatively rare. In an interview with Vancouver Province, Margaret Gee showed gratitude for the kindness shown to her by older members of the legal profession.

There were very few female lawyers in North America at the time. Being one of the very few female lawyers, Margaret Gee stated that she liked being called a "lady lawyer".
